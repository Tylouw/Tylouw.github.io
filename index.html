Dexter Surgical Robot Simulator and Teleoperation Study

For my bachelor thesis at Fraunhofer IMTE, I developed and evaluated a training simulator for the Dexter surgical robot system. The study compared unconventional input devices such as a game controller and a 3D space mouse and included a test-subject study where participants first used the simulator and then worked with the real Dexter robot system in an operating-room scenario.

Brobot - Custom 6-DoF Robot Arm

Designed and built a 6-DoF robot arm from mechanics to control software. I developed the CAD model, produced 3D-printed mechanical parts, integrated the electronics, and implemented the ESP32/C++ controller. The software includes forward and inverse kinematics, coordinated axis motion, Jacobian-based Cartesian jogging, and safety logic with e-stop considerations.

UR5e Cable Grasping Pipeline

Implemented the perception-to-execution pipeline for grasping a cable lying on a table with a UR5e. The setup used an Intel RealSense camera for sensing, cable detection with TrackDLO, ROS communication, UR-RTDE, and motion execution on the real robot. This project connects perception-based manipulation with grasping of deformable objects.

Whiteboard Drawing Robot

Developed a whiteboard drawing system using a UR robot, ROS 2, and OptiTrack, with custom calibration and robot integration for accurate drawing tasks. I derived the hand-eye calibration to estimate the transform from end effector to marker and from robot base to OptiTrack world origin using Ax=b. Continuous optical tracking lets the system adapt to changing whiteboard and pencil holder positions during drawing.

About

About me

I am from the north of Germany and currently finishing my full-degree M.Sc. in Robot Systems at the University of Southern Denmark in Odense. Before, i graduated as B.Sc. in robotics and autonomous systems at the university of Lübeck, Germany. Most of my work sits between software and real hardware. How I work
